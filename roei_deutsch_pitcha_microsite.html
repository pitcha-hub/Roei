Proof of thinking

Different industries. Same pattern.

Proof Point 01
Hummus API

Google Cloud had a branding issue.

Yeah. Google.

So I launched a brand-new API.

It was about Hummus.

Yeah. Hummus.

Turns out that's a surprisingly effective way to get people talking about cloud infrastructure.

The ripple effect was so ridiculous that MSNBC ended up covering it.

Proof Point 02
Future Store

My predecessor spent years preaching cloud migration to a retail unicorn.

The thing is they never joined the church.

Retailers aren't inspired only by infrastructure.

They're inspired by what's possible because of it.

So I offered them a vision to the holy land.

We stopped talking about migration.

And started talking about the store of the future.

The cloud got their attention.

The future store got their imagination.

They immediately wanted to convert.

The vision became a multi-million-dollar partnership with Google Cloud.

Big enough to earn its own stage at NRF in New York.

Proof Point 03
Purple Rebellion

An AI startup hired me to generate leads at one of the biggest sales conferences in the world.

There was just one problem.

We were one booth among nearly 200.

Everyone else showed up talking about agents.

I brought four agents.

Well...

Not actual agents.

Black suits. Purple Ray-Bans matching the startup's brand colors.

Their badges were armed with QR codes and loaded with free credits.

By the end of the first day, people were looking for our agents before they were looking for the booths.

Change the conversation.Create gravity.
